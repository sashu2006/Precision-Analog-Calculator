Precision Continuous-Time Analog CalculatorOverviewModern edge-device applications increasingly demand localized, on-chip processing with stringent constraints on power, memory, and latency. Traditional digital implementations struggle to compute non-linear functions efficiently, inherently requiring continuous sampling (ADCs), quantization, and memory-heavy Lookup Tables (LUTs).  This project presents a hardware-level Continuous-Time Analog Calculator that bypasses algorithmic computation entirely. By exploiting the fundamental device physics of discrete PN junctions, operational amplifiers, and optimized resistor networks, this architecture performs instantaneous, zero-latency physical computation at the speed of electron flow—completely eliminating the need for clock-driven execution.  Architectural HighlightsThe system acts as a pure analog Arithmetic Logic Unit (ALU), dynamically executing math via hardware:  Linear Operations: High-precision Analog Adder and Subtractor circuits utilizing virtual ground summing configurations.  Absolute Value: An inverting precision rectifier architecture relying on dynamic diode switching to bypass or cascade operational amplifier stages based on input polarity.  Non-Linear Operations: High-fidelity Logarithmic, Multiplier, and Divider modules. These circuits exploit the Shockley diode equation to convert linear voltages into exponential scales, converting subtraction into division via hardware.  Thermodynamic Calibration: Implemented a hardware-derived scaling factor to strip away the physical thermodynamic constraints of the diodes (compensating for the thermal voltage $V_T$ and ideality factor $\eta$), allowing extraction of the pure, unitless mathematical result.  Performance & BenchmarkingPhysical prototype testing on solderless breadboards across 32 discrete op-amps yielded exceptional mathematical accuracy:  Overall System Precision: Achieved an overall hardware system average error of 2.15%, vastly outperforming the initial 5% design constraint.  Benchmark Victory: The flagship Logarithmic Ratio circuit achieved an average error margin of just 0.95%. This represents a performance increase of over 4x when benchmarked against a comparable MIT hardware project (which reported a 4.44% error).  Core ComponentsActive Devices: LM358P Operational Amplifiers, chosen to prove high accuracy via subspace mapping despite inherent offset constraints.  Non-Linear Engines: 1N4148 Fast Switching Diodes, utilized for their strict adherence to the Shockley equation at high frequencies.
